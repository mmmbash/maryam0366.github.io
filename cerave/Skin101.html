Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I exfoliate my skin?

+

The frequency of exfoliation depends on your skin type and the type of exfoliant you're using. Generally, it's recommended to exfoliate 2-3 times a week for normal and oily skin, and once a week for sensitive or dry skin. Over-exfoliation can strip the skin of its natural oils, leading to irritation or dryness.

Can I mix different skincare ingredients?

+

While some skincare ingredients can be mixed for enhanced benefits, others might not react well together. For example, it's usually safe to combine hyaluronic acid with most ingredients, but using vitamin C with retinol or AHAs/BHAs can be too harsh for some skin types. It's important to do your research or consult with a dermatologist.

What's the correct order to apply skincare products?

+

A general rule of thumb for skincare application is to apply products from thinnest to thickest consistency. Start with cleanser, followed by toner, serum, and eye cream. Then apply treatments or spot treatments, followed by moisturizer, and lastly sunscreen in the morning.

How can I tell if a product isn't right for my skin?

+

If a product isn't suitable for your skin, you might experience signs such as redness, itching, burning, excessive dryness, or breakout. It's important to patch test new products before fully incorporating them into your routine. If irritation occurs, discontinue use and consult a dermatologist.
